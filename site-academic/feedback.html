§4 User Feedback

Two subject-matter interviews shaped the design direction

4.1 Interviewees

"The main function of drones / RC vehicles in SWAT is to reduce risk to law enforcement officers. The unit gets deployed to a strategic position inside the structure, hides itself, and looks for movement and location of the bad guys. All members of the team are trained on operating the devices."
David Kinkaid · Retired SWAT Team Commander, FBI Los Angeles
"Cost-effectiveness is the right competition to be in. Add a receiver for known signals on top of the camera so you get more data. Raspberry Pi is a robust option for a Linux dev environment, and the software should be built so the product can scale."
Kevin Vo · B.S. Aerospace Engineering, NIWC Pacific Scientist

4.2 Design changes driven by feedback

FeedbackChange
"Operator stays in the back of the formation."RC link prioritized over autonomy in v1 — direct human pilot.
"Add a receiver for known signals."Reserved expansion bay on the chassis for an SDR / signal module.
"Raspberry Pi is robust for scaling."Pi adopted as compute platform; software written modularly for future upgrades.
"All SWAT members must operate it with base training."Controls intentionally simple — no flight modes, no calibration steps.

4.3 User-feedback test plan

Bring the printed prototype with electronics installed. Provide minimal instruction. Ask:

  • How intuitive are the controls?
  • Can you turn the system on without help?
  • How does the movement feel?
  • Is the visual feedback usable?
  • What else should we improve?

Proposed Solution

A printable, modular X-frame chassis.

An open-source 3D-printed quadcopter chassis you can print at the makerspace, repair in minutes, and modify for any project.

Exploded view: chassis is the core, everything else bolts on.

The prototype

The chassis is a 250 mm wheelbase X-frame printed in PLA+ on a Prusa MK4. Each arm is a separate piece that bolts into a central hub plate, so a crashed arm is a 90-minute reprint, not a $60 replacement order.

Why a printed chassis?

$

~$4 to replace

A whole frame in filament costs less than a coffee. Even a re-print of every arm is under $2.

🔧

Modular arms

Each of the four arms is a separate part. Crash one? Print one.

📐

Tunable

STL is parametric — change wheelbase, motor pattern, or arm thickness without redesigning from scratch.

🪶

Light enough

112 g frame mass keeps total AUW under 480 g — well within the FAA recreational limit.

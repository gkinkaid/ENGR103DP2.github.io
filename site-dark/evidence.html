Test results

✓

Drop test

Dropped the assembled chassis from 1.5 m onto concrete five times. v3 frame: zero cracks. v2 frame: cracked one arm on the third drop.

✓

Flight time

5 min 40 sec hover on a 4S 1500 mAh pack — within 8% of the same components on a stock carbon frame.

✓

Vibration

Gyro noise on the flight controller measured below the noise threshold for default Betaflight filtering, indicating the printed frame doesn't introduce problematic vibration.

✓

Repair after crash

After a real crash test, replacing the broken arm took 22 minutes total (including the print). Faster than ordering a part.

How it maps back to the need

Cost — frame cost dropped from ~$65 to ~$4. ✓

Fragility — PLA+ flexes and absorbs impact instead of shattering. ✓

Repairability — single-arm reprint in <90 min vs replacing whole frame. ✓

Customization — parametric STL means anyone with Fusion 360 can adjust wheelbase, motor pattern, or payload mounts. ✓

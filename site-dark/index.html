ENGR 103 / D8 Presentation

A drone you can crash, fix, and fly again.

Skyframe is an RC quadcopter built around a custom 3D-printed chassis — designed for students who want to learn flight engineering without being scared to break it.

See the prototype → View evidence

The Need

Off-the-shelf hobby drones are expensive, fragile, and almost impossible to repair when they fail.

For students learning to fly, build, and tune RC aircraft, that combination is a problem. A single hard landing can total a $300 quadcopter, and proprietary frames mean replacement parts are slow to ship and rarely a perfect fit. The result: students avoid pushing their drones, learn slowly, and end up with a closet full of broken plastic.

$

Cost

Entry-level FPV quads start around $250–$400. A single replacement frame is $40–$80, plus shipping and downtime.

⚠

Fragility

Carbon fiber frames are stiff but shatter on impact. One bad crash can crack arms beyond repair.

↻

Repairability

Most commercial frames are sold as full kits. You can't just replace the broken arm — you replace the whole frame.

⚙

Customization

Standard frames don't fit non-standard projects. Want to mount a science payload? You're modifying with a Dremel.

Who this is for

Primary user: a college engineering student (like me) who wants to learn drone design hands-on, can't afford to keep replacing crashed frames, and has access to a 3D printer in the makerspace.

Secondary users include high-school robotics teams and university aerospace clubs that want a low-cost, modifiable platform for experiments. The chassis design is open and printable on any FDM printer with a 200×200 mm bed.

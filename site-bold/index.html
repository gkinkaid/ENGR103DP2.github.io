A drone you can crash without crying.

Skyframe is an RC quadcopter built around a custom 3D-printed chassis. Cheap to print. Easy to fix. Made for students who actually want to fly the thing.

See the build → The proof

The problem 🛒

Hobby drones cost too much, break too easily, and take forever to fix.

If you're a student who wants to learn drone engineering, the math is brutal. A starter quadcopter is $250–$400. One bad crash — and trust me, there will be a bad crash — and you might be looking at a $65 frame replacement that ships in three to five business days. So you stop pushing. You fly conservative. You learn slow.

$

Too expensive

Entry-level frames cost $40–$80. Replacement = another $40–$80 + shipping.

💥

Too fragile

Carbon fiber is stiff but shatters on impact. One hard landing and you're done.

⌛

Too slow to fix

Whole frame kits only — no single-arm replacements. Wait for shipping. Lose a weekend.

🔒

Too rigid

Want to mount a payload, camera, or sensor? You're modding with a Dremel.

Who I built this for 👀

The user: a college engineering student (like me) who wants to learn drone design hands-on, can't afford to keep replacing crashed frames, and has access to a 3D printer.

Also great for high-school robotics teams and university aerospace clubs who want a cheap, modifiable platform for class projects. The design prints on any FDM printer with a 200×200 mm bed.
